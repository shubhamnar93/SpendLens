# User Interviews

## Interview 1 — "A.N.", Chartered Accountant at a Large audit firm
---
## Direct quotes:
- “Many companies have enterprise deals with Microsoft and pay yearly fixed amounts.”
- “Employees aren’t allowed to use other AI tools because of security policies.”
- “Since the price is fixed, they just treat it as a standard software expense.”

## Most surprising Thing
Security restriction, not cost are the main reason many companies avoid multiple AI tools.

## What it changed about your desgin
This clarified that SpendLens should focus on startups, freelancers, and small teams, not enterprises with fixed vendor contracts.
---

## Interview 2 — "R.N.", Consultant at Consultancy Firm
---
## Direct quotes:
- “Consultants get a budget and decide what tools they need.”
- “There isn’t a system to track what each person subscribes to.”
- “I just remember my Claude subscription from my UPI transactions.”

## Most surprising Thing
The only “tracking system” was scrolling through payment history.

## What it changed about your desgin
This reinforced the importance of simple import of payments and automatic spend detection for later stages.
---

## Interview 3 — "J.M.", Solo founder, pre-revenue 
---
## Direct quotes:
- “I honestly don’t know what I’m paying for anymore.”
- “I don’t have any method to keep track of these subscriptions.”
- “I probably pay too little right now to even bother auditing it.”

## Most surprising Thing
The most surprising realization was that lack of tracking is intentional neglect, not lack of awareness. He knows it’s messy but doesn’t feel the problem is big enough yet to justify effort.

## What it changed about your desgin
This shifted the positioning from “serious cost optimization tool” to a lightweight, low-effort audit that feels worth using even when spending is still small.
